Investor Pitch Recommendations

A comprehensive guide to effectively communicate your value proposition to potential investors and secure pre-seed funding.

Pitch Deck Structure & Content Recommendations

1. Cover Slide

  • Title: "Lien Tracker: Transforming Personal Injury Case Management for Chiropractors"
  • Tagline: "The specialized SaaS solution for the $X billion personal injury chiropractic market"
  • Visual: Clean, professional design with subtle medical and legal imagery
  • Contact: Your name, email, phone number

2. Problem Slide

  • Focus on 3 key pain points:
    1. "Chiropractors lose 20-30% of potential revenue due to poor lien tracking and documentation"
    2. "Personal injury cases require 4x more administrative time than standard cases"
    3. "Communication gaps between chiropractors and attorneys delay settlements by months"
  • Visual: Show the current fragmented workflow with clear pain points
  • Validation: Include 1-2 quotes from chiropractors about these challenges

3. Solution Slide

  • Value proposition: "Lien Tracker is the first specialized SaaS platform that streamlines the entire personal injury case lifecycle for chiropractors"
  • Key features (limit to 3-4):
    • Case status workflow designed specifically for personal injury liens
    • Integrated document management with attorney communication tracking
    • Financial forecasting for delayed lien payments
  • Visual: Simple diagram showing how Lien Tracker connects the chiropractor, patient, and attorney

4. Market Opportunity Slide

  • Total Addressable Market: 70,000 chiropractors in the US
  • Serviceable Addressable Market: 14,000-21,000 chiropractic practices handling personal injury cases
  • Serviceable Obtainable Market: 3,000-5,000 practices in initial target states
  • Visual: Funnel graphic showing TAM → SAM → SOM
  • Key insight: "Each practice manages an average of $X in outstanding liens"

5. Competitive Advantage Slide

  • Title: "Why Lien Tracker Wins"
  • Highlight 3 key differentiators:
    1. Specialized workflow for personal injury cases vs. general practice management
    2. Dual-industry integration bridging healthcare and legal processes
    3. Financial visibility specifically designed for delayed payment model
  • Visual: 2x2 matrix positioning Lien Tracker against competitors on axes of "Specialization" and "Ease of Use"

6. Product Demo Slide

  • Title: "Lien Tracker in Action"
  • Content: 3-4 screenshots of key features:
    • Case management dashboard with status pipeline
    • Document repository with version control
    • Law firm performance metrics
  • Tip: Prepare a 60-second live demo walkthrough focusing on unique features

7. Business Model Slide

  • Pricing: $199-$299 monthly subscription per practice
  • Unit Economics:
    • Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): $1,000-$1,500
    • Lifetime Value (LTV): $7,000-$10,000 (3-year average customer lifespan)
    • LTV:CAC Ratio: 7:1
  • Visual: Simple chart showing revenue growth projections

8. Go-to-Market Strategy Slide

  • Initial Focus: California chiropractic practices with high PI caseloads
  • Acquisition Channels (limit to 3):
    • Direct outreach to practices with high PI volume
    • Strategic partnerships with attorney networks
    • Content marketing focused on lien management best practices
  • Visual: Map showing geographic expansion strategy

9. Traction Slide

  • Current Status:
    • MVP completed with core features implemented
    • X beta users providing feedback
    • Key partnerships established with [specific names if available]
  • Milestones: Timeline showing key achievements and future goals
  • Testimonials: Include 1-2 quotes from early users or advisors

10. Team Slide

  • Your Background: Emphasize your experience as a practicing chiropractor (Dr. Tino Abon, Magnolia Chiropractic)
  • Key Insight: Highlight that you've experienced these pain points firsthand
  • Advisors: Include any industry experts or advisors supporting the project
  • Future Hires: Mention 2-3 key roles you plan to fill with funding

11. Financial Projections Slide

  • 3-Year Forecast:
    • Year 1: 50-100 customers, $100K-$300K ARR
    • Year 2: 300-500 customers, $600K-$1.5M ARR
    • Year 3: 1,000+ customers, $2M-$4M ARR
  • Visual: Simple bar chart showing revenue growth
  • Key Metrics: Highlight CAC, churn rate, and gross margin

12. Funding Ask Slide

  • Amount: $250K pre-seed funding
  • Terms: 5% equity with 3-year fixed term
  • Use of Funds:
    • 40% Product Development
    • 35% Sales & Marketing
    • 15% Customer Success
    • 10% Operations
  • Milestones: What specific goals will this funding help you achieve?

13. Vision/Closing Slide

  • Big Picture: "Lien Tracker will become the industry standard for personal injury case management, processing over $X billion in liens annually"
  • Impact: How you'll transform practice efficiency and profitability
  • Call to Action: Clear next steps for interested investors

Pitch Delivery Recommendations

Preparation

  1. Practice the 5-minute version: Most pitch opportunities will be brief
  2. Practice the 15-minute version: For more formal pitch meetings
  3. Prepare for the most common questions:
    • How will you compete with larger practice management systems?
    • What's your customer acquisition strategy?
    • How will you scale beyond your initial market?
    • What's your personal experience with this problem?

Storytelling Elements

  1. Open with a personal story: Share a specific example from your practice about a personal injury case that was mismanaged due to poor tracking
  2. Use concrete numbers: "Chiropractors spend an average of X hours per week on lien paperwork"
  3. Paint the vision: Describe how Lien Tracker transforms the daily operations of a chiropractic practice

Visual Presentation Tips

  1. Limit text: No more than 15-20 words per slide
  2. Use consistent design: Professional, clean template with medical/legal elements
  3. Include real screenshots: Show the actual product you've built
  4. Use charts for data: Make market size and financial projections visual

Demo Recommendations

  1. Keep it brief: 60-90 seconds maximum
  2. Focus on differentiators: Show features unique to personal injury cases
  3. Prepare a backup: Have screenshots ready in case of technical issues
  4. Tell a story: Walk through a typical case journey rather than just features

Handling Investor Questions

  1. Prepare for objections: Practice responses to concerns about market size, competition, and scaling
  2. Know your numbers: Be ready to discuss CAC, LTV, churn, and unit economics
  3. Be honest about challenges: Acknowledge risks but show how you'll mitigate them
  4. Follow up plan: Have a clear process for post-pitch engagement

Supporting Materials to Prepare

One-Page Executive Summary

  • Create a concise summary that investors can reference after your pitch
  • Include key metrics, value proposition, and contact information
  • Design it to be easily shareable

Financial Model

  • Develop a detailed spreadsheet with monthly projections for 3 years
  • Include key assumptions about customer acquisition, churn, and pricing
  • Be prepared to share this after initial interest

Competitive Analysis Document

  • Create a detailed comparison of Lien Tracker vs. alternatives
  • Include feature comparison, pricing, and target market differences
  • Use this to address competition questions in detail

Customer Testimonials

  • Collect quotes from beta users or potential customers
  • Include specific pain points your solution addresses
  • Use these throughout your pitch to validate the problem

Investor Meeting Strategy

Before the Meeting

  1. Research each investor: Understand their portfolio, investment thesis, and background
  2. Customize your pitch: Emphasize elements most relevant to each investor
  3. Prepare specific questions: Show you've done your homework

During the Meeting

  1. Listen actively: Pay attention to what aspects generate the most interest
  2. Be concise: Respect time limits and leave room for questions
  3. Show passion: Demonstrate your commitment to solving this problem
  4. Be adaptable: Be willing to jump to sections that interest the investor most

After the Meeting

  1. Follow up within 24 hours: Send a thank you and any promised materials
  2. Address questions: Provide thoughtful responses to any open items
  3. Maintain communication: Share meaningful updates on progress
  4. Ask for introductions: Request connections to other potential investors

Pitch Practice Schedule

Week 1-2: Content Development

  • Draft all slides based on recommendations
  • Create supporting visuals and charts
  • Develop key talking points for each slide

Week 3: Internal Rehearsal

  • Practice full presentation multiple times
  • Record yourself and review for improvements
  • Refine timing and transitions

Week 4: Feedback Sessions

  • Present to 3-5 trusted advisors or colleagues
  • Collect specific feedback on content and delivery
  • Make final adjustments based on feedback
